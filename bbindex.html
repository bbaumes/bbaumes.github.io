case 01

Rebuilding a service model from inside it

SelectMinds
New York
2008–2013

The model was two people per book — an account manager and a service manager. It was rigid, it didn't scale, and I was a CSM with no mandate to change any of it.

In 2011 I wrote it up anyway and took it to the C-suite: split the roles. Build a dedicated product specialist team to carry the technical depth, and free the account managers for the relationship and commercial work they were actually good at. Nobody had asked for it.

They ran with it — and built the re-org around me. I moved into a new hybrid role that hadn't existed before: I stood up and managed the specialist team, kept a small book of top-tier clients so I never lost the thread of the actual work, and sat part-time with product as the Voice of the Customer, feeding what we heard into roadmap and prioritization. The model held all the way through the Oracle acquisition.

Same instinct, a second time: I noticed clients struggling with something we didn't sell, pitched a Community Management service, and grew it into a billable three-person function.

Margin note — where it came from When I started, the company was on two floors: client services upstairs, product and dev downstairs, and a cold war on the staircase. I noticed the downstairs team ate lunch together, so I started showing up. Suspicious for a while, then not. That's where the wall came down — not in a meeting, at lunch. The restructure worked because those relationships were already there. I still talk to some of them, fifteen years on.
